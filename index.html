About

I am an AI research director at J.P. Morgan AI Research. My work centers on synthetic data and generative AI, time series, and behavior modeling across a range of projects.

Current and ongoing work includes synthetic personas for credit-card marketing, behavior modeling to build personalized co-pilots for front-office teams, synthetic data for AML and fraud detection, time-series modeling — including using synthetic data to train time-series foundation models — and generative-AI agents for market simulation. A growing thread runs through all of it: using synthetic data to power agentic evaluations, stress-testing and benchmarking AI agents with controllable, privacy-safe data.

Before J.P. Morgan, I was a researcher at the Comcast AI group and the Rutgers Discovery Informatics Institute.
